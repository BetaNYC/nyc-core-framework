The NYC Core Framework's dymnamic color system allows you to intuitively manage colors and apply them with little effort across pages, components, and content.

Color Accessibility

When selecting colors for your theme, it is wise to check that your colors meet a sufficient contrast ratio.

In the NYC Core Framework white is treated as a color and it serves as your default theme color. In our templates and examples, you may have noticed the <body> tag has a .bc-white class applied (Figure 1).

Background Color Classes

Our background color classes make it easy to apply color to an element, without needing to apply colors to that elements (or child elements) text and borders. Each theme color (e.g. primary) is assigned predesignated text color, link color, and border color.
