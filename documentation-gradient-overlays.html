Gradient Overlays

Gradient overlay classes allow you to easily add translucent gradients over the top of images. Because they are made using psuedo elements, gradient overlay layer can be hidden or shown at specific breakpoints. See Gradient Overlays with Featured Panels.

Make Your Own Gradients

Want to make your own gradients? Head over to scss/_f-ui-elements.scss and scroll to E. Gradients. Here you will find a few examples that you can modify—or copy and paste one of them to make your own.

The class that you create here will be used in your html. Name it whatever you want, of course. For the sake of argument, try .gradient-overlay-{direction}-{color} where direction is x or y, and color is black, white, dark, light, or sunset.

Gradient Overlays call a mixin from scss/_c-custom-mixins.scss under C. Linear gradient. These mixins were created in the spirit of simplicity. Feel free to modify them, or replace them to your liking.
